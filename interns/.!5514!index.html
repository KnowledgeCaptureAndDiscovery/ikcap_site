Summer Internships in Artificial Intelligence at the
Interactive Knowledge Capture Group

We welcome graduate, undergraduate, and high-school students to join our research group as Summer Interns. Each year, we design projects that are suitable for the background and interests of each selected student.

View of the USC Information Sciences Institute (tall building on the right) from the Marina del Rey channel, with the Santa Monica Mountains in the background

Since our Summer Internships started in 1999, we have welcome undergraduate students from Stanford University, MIT, Carnegie Mellon University, Brown University, and many other institutions. We have worked with high-school students in the Los Angeles area and in some cases beyond through remote participation.

Our students have worked in a wide range of topics ranging from artificial intelligence to semantic web, from data collection to data science, from geosciences to medicine, from research to education. Here are some overviews of our most recent Summer Internships
